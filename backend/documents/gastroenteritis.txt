Gastroenteritis (Stomach Flu) - Patient Information Guide

What is Gastroenteritis?
Gastroenteritis is an inflammation of the stomach and intestines, usually caused by a viral or bacterial infection. It is commonly known as "stomach flu," though it is unrelated to influenza.

Common Symptoms
- Watery diarrhea
- Nausea and vomiting
- Abdominal cramps and pain
- Low-grade fever
- Headache and muscle aches

Causes
- Viral infections (norovirus, rotavirus) are the most common cause.
- Bacterial infections from contaminated food or water (Salmonella, E. coli).
- Parasites in rare cases.

Diagnosis
Most cases are diagnosed based on symptoms. Stool tests may be ordered in the General Medicine department if symptoms are severe, prolonged, or if there is blood in the stool.

Management
- Stay hydrated with oral rehydration solutions or clear fluids.
- Eat bland, easy-to-digest foods as tolerated (rice, bananas, toast).
- Avoid dairy, caffeine, and fatty foods until recovered.
- Antibiotics are only used for confirmed bacterial infections.
- Most cases resolve within 1-3 days without treatment.

When to Seek Emergency Care
Seek immediate care for signs of severe dehydration (dizziness, dry mouth, little or no urination), blood in vomit or stool, or high fever lasting more than 3 days.
