Common Cold and Influenza (Flu) - Patient Information Guide

What is the Difference?
The common cold and influenza (flu) are both respiratory illnesses caused by different viruses. Flu symptoms are usually more severe and come on more suddenly than cold symptoms.

Common Cold Symptoms
- Runny or stuffy nose
- Sneezing
- Sore throat
- Mild cough
- Low or no fever

Influenza (Flu) Symptoms
- High fever, often sudden onset
- Body aches and chills
- Fatigue and weakness
- Dry cough
- Headache

Diagnosis
Most colds are diagnosed based on symptoms alone. Flu can be confirmed with a rapid influenza diagnostic test available at the outpatient department.

Management
- Rest and stay well hydrated.
- Over-the-counter medications can relieve fever, congestion, and pain.
- Antiviral medications may be prescribed for flu if started within 48 hours of symptom onset, particularly for high-risk patients.
- Most colds resolve on their own within 7-10 days.

When to Seek Medical Care
Visit the General Medicine outpatient clinic if symptoms last more than 10 days, or seek emergency care for difficulty breathing, chest pain, persistent high fever, or severe dehydration.

Prevention
Annual flu vaccination, frequent handwashing, and avoiding close contact with sick individuals are recommended.
