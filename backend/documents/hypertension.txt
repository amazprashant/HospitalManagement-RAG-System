Hypertension (High Blood Pressure) - Patient Information Guide

What is Hypertension?
Hypertension is a condition in which the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high. It is often called the "silent killer" because it usually has no symptoms until it causes serious damage.

Common Symptoms
Most patients have no symptoms. In severe cases, symptoms may include:
- Headaches
- Shortness of breath
- Nosebleeds
- Dizziness

Diagnosis
Blood pressure is measured using a sphygmomanometer. A reading consistently at or above 140/90 mmHg is generally classified as hypertension. Readings between 120-139/80-89 mmHg are considered elevated or pre-hypertension.

Management
- Reduce salt intake and maintain a balanced diet (DASH diet recommended).
- Regular physical activity, at least 30 minutes most days of the week.
- Limit alcohol consumption and avoid smoking.
- Medications such as ACE inhibitors, diuretics, or beta-blockers may be prescribed by the Cardiology department.
- Routine blood pressure checks should be done at every hospital visit.

Complications if Untreated
Uncontrolled hypertension increases risk of heart attack, stroke, kidney failure, and vision loss.

When to Seek Emergency Care
Seek emergency care immediately if blood pressure exceeds 180/120 mmHg accompanied by chest pain, severe headache, or difficulty speaking.
