Asthma - Patient Information Guide

What is Asthma?
Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition in which the airways become inflamed, narrow, and produce extra mucus, making breathing difficult. It can affect people of all ages but often begins in childhood.

Common Symptoms
- Wheezing
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness
- Coughing, especially at night or early morning

Common Triggers
- Allergens such as pollen, dust mites, and pet dander
- Cold air or sudden temperature changes
- Respiratory infections
- Exercise
- Smoke and air pollution

Diagnosis
Diagnosis typically involves a physical exam, lung function tests (spirometry), and a review of symptom history. The Pulmonology department handles asthma diagnosis and management.

Management
- Use of a quick-relief (rescue) inhaler during flare-ups.
- Daily controller medications (inhaled corticosteroids) for moderate to severe asthma.
- Identifying and avoiding personal triggers.
- Creating a written asthma action plan with your doctor.

When to Seek Emergency Care
Go to the Emergency Department immediately if you experience severe shortness of breath, bluish lips or face, or if your rescue inhaler is not providing relief.
