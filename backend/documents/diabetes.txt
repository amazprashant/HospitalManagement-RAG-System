Diabetes Mellitus - Patient Information Guide

What is Diabetes?
Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar (glucose) levels, either because it doesn't produce enough insulin (Type 1) or doesn't use insulin effectively (Type 2).

Common Symptoms
- Frequent urination
- Excessive thirst
- Unexplained weight loss
- Fatigue and blurred vision
- Slow-healing wounds

Diagnosis
Diagnosis is typically made through fasting blood glucose tests, HbA1c tests, or an oral glucose tolerance test. Patients with a fasting glucose above 126 mg/dL on two separate occasions are generally diagnosed with diabetes.

Management
- Type 1 diabetes requires lifelong insulin therapy.
- Type 2 diabetes is managed with lifestyle changes (diet, exercise), oral medications, and sometimes insulin.
- Regular blood sugar monitoring is essential for both types.
- Patients should attend the Endocrinology outpatient clinic every 3 months for follow-up.

Complications if Untreated
Poorly controlled diabetes can lead to nerve damage, kidney disease, vision loss, and increased risk of heart disease and stroke.

When to Seek Emergency Care
Seek immediate medical attention for symptoms of very high or very low blood sugar, such as confusion, fainting, rapid breathing, or fruity-smelling breath.
